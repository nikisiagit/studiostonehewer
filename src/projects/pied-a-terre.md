intro_text_2: >-
  This 1920s west-side apartment has unique qualities, with its structured
  ceiling and the inflow of light through low-hung windows creating a
  breathable, open space. Delivered entirely as a virtual design, we mapped the
  home room by room, building a custom plan of moodboards, 3D elevations, and
  shopping lists to be implemented on the client's own time and budget.


  Inspired by the history of great pattern artists such as William Morris, and the evolving design trends of the coming year, we chose neutral but bold base tones while being purposeful with print and vintage flair. The palette was curated with intentionality, keeping in mind colours that are consistent and collaborative. Textural elements included heavy linens, subtle stripes, woven fibers, glass, wood, and vintage reclamation to add feeling, interest, and character, providing juxtaposition to the modern pieces in the space.


  The aim was a retreat that is open and versatile: living and dining designed as separate spaces but with twinned elements such as colour and wood tones, bringing them together to accommodate everything from relaxing evenings to dinner parties, game nights, and work sessions. High-performance fabrics and finishes were specified to survive the wear of remote working and living without compromising on design flair.


  Built-in shelving structures house curiosa, sentiments of life, and moments of love, while versatile choices such as a vintage console over a modern cabinet and a round table connected to white oak shelving keep the home flexible to the client's evolving needs. The result is a space that feels effortlessly intentional, curated with daily rituals in mind.
